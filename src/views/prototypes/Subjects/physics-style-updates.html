Scientific skills for life

A degree in Physics will help you develop skills for life. You’ll learn how to identify a problem, come up with a theory to explain it, and then test that theory rigorously. Learning through curiosity

Workload

To achieve an average grade in a course, you should plan to spend a minimum of 10 hours per point. So, for one 15-point course you should allow for 150 hours of work over the trimester. That’s about 10–12 hours per week for each course, including lectures, tutorials, labs, and exam periods.

If you are studying full-time, you are likely to take six to eight courses over the year. Plan to balance your study, work, and life with this workload in mind.

Choosing course

If you are taking a three-year degree, it’s a good idea to look ahead and think about what you might like to study in your second and third years, as your chosen major or majors may have specific prerequisites for advanced-level courses.

To add variety and depth to your degree, you might take some courses from outside the sciences.
