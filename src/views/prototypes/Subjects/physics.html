Scientific skills for life

A degree in Physics will help you develop skills for life. You’ll learn how to identify a problem, come up with a theory to explain it, and then test that theory rigorously. You’ll develop your confidence in mathematics and learn to write computer programs. And as making new discoveries is a team effort, you’ll learn to collaborate with others.

Working from home

In an uncertain world, it helps to know that your learning is supported wherever you are. If you’re not able to work on campus, you can access video lectures, live online tutorials, and remote lab sessions.

Find out more about studying remotely

Improve your writing

Writing is a key part of science. Throughout your degree study, you’ll develop the skills to write for a range of audiences. Whether it’s a lab report for an academic audience or a presentation to a sponsor, you’ll be able to share your discoveries and deliver your message clearly.
